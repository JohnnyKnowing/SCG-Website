=== HOW WE HELP SECTION ===
How We Help

We take the time to actually know them.

=== HOW WE HELP - THE 5 STEPS ===
Step 1: Get to know them
Before knowing how to help, we need to know the person. How they think. What matters to them. Where they feel most like themselves and where they feel furthest from it.

Step 2: Build a real relationship
We give the relationship time to root. We sit outside family, school, and peer life. A space where the parts of themselves they are still figuring out how to hold can come forward safely.

Step 3: Shape the support to fit
Once we understand how someone works, we build from there. Mentoring, skill building, self-exploration, all of it tuned to this specific young person.

Step 4: Develop identity and purpose
The heart of the work. Helping a young person settle into who they are, find their own motivation, and build a sense of self that holds over time.

Step 5: Address what gets in the way
When anxiety, depression, or other struggles are blocking growth, we address them as part of the larger picture, in service of the foundation we are building together.

=== FAQ Q&A LIST ===
Question: How does your collaborative care model work?
Answer: Johnny Spangler, our Founder, acts as the Clinical Director and supervisor. He actively coordinates on case planning and hosts weekly clinical supervision. This means your teen receives dedicated relational focus while being backed by extensive clinical expertise.

Question: My teen does not want therapy. Can this still work?
Answer: Often, yes. Most teens resist being evaluated or "fixed." We specialize in setting a warm, non-judgmental atmosphere outside the pressure of family or school dynamics, helping them feel met on their own terms. This usually melts therapeutic resistance quickly.

Question: Did I cause this?
Answer: Almost never in the way you fear. Young people usually struggle because of a friction mismatch between their specific developmental path and the complex environments around them. Asking the question simply highlights how deeply you care.

Question: What if there are things they cannot say to us at home?
Answer: That is a natural, healthy milestone of adolescent development. Developing an independent inner life is healthy. We offer a secure, objective container outside the home where they can explore hard questions safely, bringing clarity back to their relationship with you.

Question: How do you approach neurodiversity?
Answer: We work through a strictly neurodiversity-affirming lens. Whether carrying a diagnosis (ADHD, Autism, Executive Dysfunction) or navigating general learning/processing differences, we focus on identifying their specific processing profile and building tailored skills rather than attempting to enforce behavioral assimilation.

Question: How do we get started?
Answer: A simple consultation call. It is a warm, low-pressure conversation to discuss your child's background, explain our fit, and connect you with a clinician if appropriate.

=== TASK 2: GETTING YOUR NPI-1 ===
Task 02
Applying for your NPI-1 Number

The National Provider Identifier (NPI) is a unique 10-digit identification number issued by the federal government (CMS) to healthcare providers. As an individual therapist, you need an NPI Type 1 (Individual) number. This number stays with you throughout your entire career and is required by HIPAA for all insurance billing, records, and credentialing systems.

Taxonomy Code for Mental Health Associates:
During the application, you will be asked to select your Provider Taxonomy. For most graduates entering associate counseling, the standard taxonomy code is 101YM0800X (Mental Health Counselor) or 106H00000X (Marriage & Family Therapist).

Step-by-Step NPI-1 Guide:
1. Create an NPPES Account
Visit the National Plan and Provider Enumeration System (NPPES) website. Create an I&A (Identity & Access) account to login.
2. Apply for an Individual NPI
Select "Apply for an NPI for myself (Type 1 Individual Provider)". Follow the application pages: enter your legal name, social security number, contact details, and graduation/practice location.
3. Enter Practice Information & Taxonomy
Use your residential or private office address for mailing details. Select the appropriate Counselor/Therapist taxonomy code and link it to your pending or active Washington state license.
4. Receive & Save Your NPI-1
Once submitted, CMS usually issues your 10-digit NPI via email within 24 to 48 hours. Keep this email safe, as you will need to input the NPI on Task 4.

Mark Task as Completed

Task 03 - FUTURE REFERENCE ONLY
Setting Up Your CAQH ProView Profile (Not Required Yet)

Important Note: Not Required for Onboarding Right Now
Spangler Counseling Group is not currently set up to accept commercial insurance. Consequently, registering with CAQH is not required to start your employment and is provided here purely for future reference. You do not need to perform these steps today.

When the practice expands to accept insurance panels in the future, CAQH (Council for Affordable Quality Healthcare) will be used as our secure online database for credentialing. It allows you to enter your background, transcripts, licenses, and references once, which insurance carriers then use to verify your credentials.

Why CAQH Will Be Used in the Future:
When we transition to an insurance model, we will use your CAQH profile to add you to panels (such as Premera, Regence, Kaiser, and Optum). We are keeping this guide here so you have a seamless, step-by-step walkthrough ready to go when that day comes.

Future Reference: Step-by-Step CAQH Setup:
1. Register for a CAQH ProView Account
Register as a new provider on the CAQH ProView portal. You will need your active State License Number or your newly received NPI-1 to initialize registration.
2. Complete the Core Profile Modules
Fill out all biographical details, clinical education history, active licenses, insurance certifications, and CV details. This is detailed and can take up to an hour.
3. Upload Required Documents
Upload clean PDF copies of your graduation diploma/transcripts, Washington State associate counselor license certificate, professional liability insurance certificate, and a signed W9 form.
4. Authorize Spangler Counseling Group
Navigate to the "Authorize" tab within your CAQH dashboard. Make sure you set permissions to Global Authorization or specifically search and authorize Spangler Counseling Group so we can access your credentials for paneling.
